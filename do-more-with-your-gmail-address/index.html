Do more with your gmail address

There are some neat tricks built into your gmail address. It seems that when you pick up “username@gmail.com” you get more than that, a lot more !

You could append a plus (“+”) sign and any combination of words or numbers after your email address. For example, if your mail was foobar@gmail.com, you could send mail to foobar+cv@gmail.com or foobar+projectName@gmail.com and still receive the mails in your inbox.

Another trick is that you can insert one or several dots (‘.’) anywhere in your email address and still receive the mails. soo foo.bar@gmail is the same as foobar@gmail.com , neat !

It’s great for Testers and also for mail management, I can create filters for an email like foobar+blog@gmail.com if i want all mails from blog to be marked as important.

Or if I don’t trust a website , I could use foobar+domain@gmail.com, so any newsletters I get from them or from any number of other websites they sell my email address to, I can filter and delete automatically.
